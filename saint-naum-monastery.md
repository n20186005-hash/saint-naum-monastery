【历史地位 / Historical Status】

由保加利亚沙皇鲍里斯一世赞助，中世纪学者及传教士圣诺姆（St. Naum of Ohrid）于公元 905 年亲自建立。它是斯拉夫东正教最古老、最重要的文化与宗教中心之一，也是西里尔字母早期传播的核心地带。圣诺姆本人于 910 年安葬于此。/ Founded in 905 by St. Naum of Ohrid, a medieval scholar and missionary, with the support of Bulgarian Tsar Boris I. It is one of the oldest and most important spiritual and cultural centers of Slavic Orthodoxy, and a hub for the early spread of the Cyrillic script. St. Naum was buried here in 910.

【建筑特色 / Architectural Features】

修道院主体采用典型的拜占庭式建筑风格，红砖与白石交替砌筑。内部拥有令人惊叹的 16 至 19 世纪湿壁画，色彩浓郁，保存完好。修道院外部被葱郁的花园环绕，散养着数十只蓝绿色的孔雀。/ The main structure features classic Byzantine architecture with alternating red bricks and white stones. The interior boasts stunning, well-preserved frescoes from the 16th to 19th centuries. The exterior is surrounded by lush gardens where dozens of vibrant blue-green peacocks roam freely.

【建筑风格 / Architectural Style】

融合了早期拜占庭风格与马其顿中世纪地方特色。教堂建筑呈现多穹顶的十字形结构，外观古朴厚重，内部空间由于点燃的蜡烛和繁复的壁画显得神秘而神圣。/ A fusion of early Byzantine style and medieval Macedonian local characteristics. The church features a multi-domed cruciform structure. The exterior is rustic and substantial, while the interior feels mystical and sacred due to lit candles and intricate frescoes.

【文化价值 / Cultural Value】

这里是马其顿乃至整个巴尔干半岛的朝圣地。相传将耳朵贴在教堂内圣诺姆的石棺上，至今仍能听到这位圣人微弱的心跳声（实际上可能是由于下方地下泉水的震动）。它象征着信仰的奇迹与大自然力量的结合。/ A major pilgrimage site in Macedonia and the Balkans. Legend has it that if you press your ear to St. Naum's stone coffin inside the church, you can still hear his heartbeat (likely the sound of underground springs). It symbolizes the union of religious miracles and the power of nature.

【核心简介 / Core Introduction】

圣诺姆修道院不仅仅是一座宗教建筑，它更像是一个遗世独立的童话庄园。它坐落在奥赫里德湖的最南端，一边是蔚蓝如海的广阔湖面，另一边是清澈见底的黑德林河（Black Drin）泉水发源地。/ St. Naum Monastery is more than just a religious building; it feels like an isolated fairytale estate. It is perched at the southernmost tip of Lake Ohrid, flanked by the vast, ocean-blue lake on one side and the crystal-clear springs of the Black Drin river on the other.

当你踏入修道院的大门，首先迎接你的是在古树与繁花间悠然散步的孔雀，偶尔传来的孔雀啼鸣打破了山林的宁静。走进光线幽暗的红砖小教堂，抬头是历经沧桑却依然鲜艳的拜占庭壁画，空气中弥漫着淡淡的蜂蜡与乳香的味道。/ Upon entering the gates, you are greeted by peacocks strolling leisurely among ancient trees and flowers, their occasional calls breaking the forest's silence. Step into the dimly lit red-brick church, look up at the vibrant, time-worn Byzantine frescoes, and breathe in the faint scent of beeswax and frankincense.

修道院外，你可以租一艘手摇木船，在如绿宝石般透明的泉水区域缓缓穿行，看水底的泉眼“咕噜咕噜”地冒出纯净的水泡。在这里，宗教的神圣感、历史的厚重感与自然景观的极致纯净完美交融，让人彻底忘却世俗的烦恼。/ Outside, you can rent a wooden rowboat to glide gently over the emerald-transparent spring waters, watching pure bubbles rise from the underwater sources. 【历史背景 / Historical Background】

公元 9 世纪末，圣诺姆与圣克莱门特（St. Clement）一起，在奥赫里德地区大力推广斯拉夫语和东正教。905 年，圣诺姆在此地建立了这座修道院。/ In the late 9th century, St. Naum, along with St. Clement, heavily promoted the Slavic language and Orthodoxy in the Ohrid region. In 905, he founded this monastery.

在随后的几个世纪里，修道院多次被毁又多次重建。目前的教堂主体主要是在 16 世纪（奥斯曼帝国时期）于原址上重建的，而内部那些令人惊艳的壁画则大多绘制于 18 世纪和 19 世纪。由于其独特的地理位置和泉水资源，这里也曾是重要的医疗与精神疗愈中心，许多患有精神疾病的人曾被送到这里寻求圣人的庇护与治愈。/ Over the following centuries, it was destroyed and rebuilt several times. The current main church was largely reconstructed in the 16th century (during the Ottoman era) on the original foundations, with most frescoes painted in the 18th and 19th centuries. Due to its location and springs, it was also a center for physical and mental healing; many with mental illnesses were brought here to seek the saint's protection.
